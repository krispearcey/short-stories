By seven the road was gone. Not closed, not snowed in, not washed out. Gone.

The fog came in from the water before dawn and sat down over the asphalt with such complete authority that the shoulder lights disappeared first, then the painted lines, then the idea of distance itself. By breakfast, people at the station spoke about the highway the way they spoke about ships after a storm.

No one drove into it twice.

## The Last Exit

Near the overpass, a car idled with both doors open and no one inside. The radio kept playing softly to the empty ditch. Someone said a man had stepped out only long enough to be sick and had not come back when his wife called his name.

She said the fog answered her in his voice.

By afternoon the town had placed barricades at either end. Even then, everyone kept glancing toward the white wall, certain they could hear tires moving somewhere inside it.
