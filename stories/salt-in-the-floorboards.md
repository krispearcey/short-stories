The house made its first sound just after midnight, a slow inhale from somewhere under the kitchen floor. Ava sat upright on the borrowed couch and listened for pipes, wind, old timber settling.

But the second sound was unmistakable.

It was breathing.

The rental stood three minutes from the water and carried the sea indoors in small, indecent ways. Salt furred the corners of the windows. The spoons in the drawer tasted faintly metallic. Every room smelled like a sweater dried on a line too close to the surf.

## Boards

When she finally pried up the warped plank beneath the table, she found only darkness, damp insulation, and a drift of packed white crystals beneath the beams.

Salt, yes—but there were footprints in it.
