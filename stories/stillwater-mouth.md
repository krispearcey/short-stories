Nobody in the cove would tell you not to fish the pond. They would only ask why you wanted to.

Elias arrived with a tackle box, a thermos, and the kind of certainty city people mistake for preparedness. The pond was smaller than he'd imagined, ringed by tuckamore and a silence so complete that even his boots seemed embarrassed to touch the moss.

The first fish he hooked did not pull away.

It came toward him.

## Shoreline

By the time he understood that the line was guiding something rather than catching it, the floatplane that had dropped him off was long gone and the pond had gone mirror-flat. He could see his own face in it, though he was standing in shadow.
