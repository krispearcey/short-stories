About the author

Stories shaped by silence,
distance, and the things
that stay just out of sight.

Kristopher Pearcey writes liminal and cosmic horror rooted in the woods, the coastlines, and the quieter places of Newfoundland. His work leans toward slow dread, uneasy atmosphere, and the sense that something has shifted, even when nothing visible has changed.

His stories are interested in what lingers beneath ordinary life: the wrong turn that feels noticed, the room that seems smaller on the way out, the silence that stops feeling empty. Rather than rushing toward explanation, they tend to stay with uncertainty — and let it grow.

Quiet Places is a collection built from those spaces: cabins, ponds, tree lines, hallways, thresholds, and all the places that should feel familiar but do not.
